Google Talkback

Google Talkback is a developer tool that browsers offer to help those who are visually impaired. Talkback is an accessibility service that helps vision-impaired users interact with their devices. What it does is it uses spoken word, vibration and other audible feedback to let the user know what is currently on their screen, what they're touching, and what they can do with it. Google Talkback is automatically installed in Android devices when they are first purchased. Talkback uses spoken words, vibration, and other audible feedback to let the user know what is currently on their screen. As long as the user follows the prompts given from the device the user can do anything that is available on the Android.



Other Developer Tools (DevTools) that Google offers are:


  • Switch Access
    Is a way to control your phone without using the touchscreen.
  • Select to Speak
    Reads anything on the screen aloud by simply the tap of the screen.
  • Trackball/Directional Pad
    This navigation will assist those who are visually impaired to augment their experience using their device.
  • Android Accessibility Suite
    Google has renamed their Talkback app to Android Accessiblity Suite, which not only contains the above listed items but it includes many others as well.
